C. E. Shannon: father of information age

Mathematician Ph.D. in Mathematics from MIT. Worked at AT&T Bell Labs and RLE in MIT Electrical engineer Mater's Thesis: electrical applications of Boolean algebra could construct any logical, numerical relationship Cryptographer “A Mathematical Theory of Cryptography," 1949. Friend of Turing For two months early in 1943, Shannon came into contact with the leading British mathematician Alan Turing. Shannon and Turing met at teatime in the cafeteria. Turing showed Shannon his 1936 paper that defined what is now known as the “Universal Turing machine"
